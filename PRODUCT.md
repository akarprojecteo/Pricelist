# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Indonesian high school students, university students, student organizations, event committees, and small communities who need affordable help with academic work, documents, graphic design, and light event support. They typically browse on mobile between classes or in group chats, compare prices quickly, and reach out via Instagram DM or WhatsApp when something fits their budget and deadline.

## Product Purpose

Akar Project is a student-subsidized services brand offering academic assistance, document and administration support, graphic design, and event coordination at micro-prices. This site is the public rate card — the primary artifact prospects see, share, and trust before ordering. Success means a reader understands what is offered, what it costs, how to order, and feels confident enough to start a free consultation.

## Brand Personality

Professional, friendly, trustworthy. The voice is calm and capable — like a reliable senior who explains things clearly without talking down. Premium in craft, not pretentious in tone. Warm enough for students; structured enough for committees handling budgets and deadlines.

## Anti-references

- AI-generated landing page aesthetics (generic card grids, decorative eyebrows on every section, glass blur nav, cream-on-cream sameness)
- Glassmorphism, cyberpunk, neon, heavy glow, large shadows, oversaturated color
- Purple gradients, blue SaaS palette, generic dashboard UI
- Nested cards, everything centered, too many borders, boring symmetrical layouts
- Inter as the primary typeface
- Visual noise that obscures pricing and policy clarity

## Design Principles

- **Clarity before decoration** — pricing, inclusions, and order steps must scan in seconds; ornament never competes with information.
- **Premium through restraint** — editorial typography, generous whitespace, and precise hierarchy instead of shadows, glow, or card stacking.
- **Trust is visible** — policies, revision limits, payment flow, and academic integrity boundaries are stated plainly, not buried.
- **Student-realistic** — copy and structure acknowledge tight budgets and deadlines without sounding cheap or desperate.
- **One surface, one job** — each view (services, bundles, order flow, policies) has a single clear outcome; no app chrome pretending to be a product dashboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for text contrast, focus visibility, and touch targets. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for tab changes and scroll behavior. Body copy in Indonesian; structure should remain parseable for screen readers (semantic headings, table headers, button labels). No reliance on color alone for status or emphasis.
